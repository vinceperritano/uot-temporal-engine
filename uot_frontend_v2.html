Unified Observer Theory · Temporal Extrapolation Engine

A structured foresight tool
for observer-guided scenario exploration.

The UOT Temporal Extrapolation Engine is an observer-guided scenario system for exploring how present events may unfold into future timelines. It gathers sources, extracts candidate events, maps causal relationships, identifies unresolved branches, and presents the resulting possibility field for human review before simulation.

The engine is inspired by the Unified Observer Theory — a novel theoretical framework that treats observation, information, causality, consciousness, and temporal possibility as deeply connected. In this app, the observer is not a passive user: your measurement basis shapes which patterns are emphasized, which uncertainties are reviewed, and how the final timeline is interpreted.

You can use the engine to explore political scenarios, institutional risks, historical turning points, geopolitical developments, personal decision timelines, research questions, futures studies, fiction and worldbuilding, or any situation where events, choices, uncertainty, and causal pressure interact over time.

This is not a prophecy machine. It is a structured foresight tool — a way to examine uncertainty, evidence, causal pressure, and branching futures with transparency and care.

Epistemic notice: The engine does not claim certainty. It models possible futures from available evidence, observer-selected priorities, and explicit causal assumptions. Every result should be read as a structured interpretation of uncertainty, not as a guaranteed outcome.
